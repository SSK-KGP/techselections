About



Maharashtra, located in western India, is the second-most populous state and a key economic hub. Its diverse geography includes mountains, coastlines, and plateaus, with major cities like Mumbai, a financial and entertainment center, and Pune, known for education and culture. Rich in history, Maharashtra was home to the Maratha Empire founded by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and played a significant role in India's independence. The state is culturally vibrant, celebrating festivals like Ganesh Chaturthi and Gudi Padwa, and is known for its traditional dances and cuisine, which includes spicy curries and snacks. Economically, Maharashtra thrives in industries like IT, manufacturing, and agriculture. For tourists, it offers a range of attractions, from historical forts and ancient caves like Ajanta, Ellora and Elephanta to scenic hill stations such as Mahabaleshwar and Lonavala, and beautiful beaches along the Konkan coast. It also has the Gateway of India.
